Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about DTF transfers, how to apply them, and what makes ours different.

What is DTF printing?

DTF stands for Direct-to-Film. We print your design onto a special PET film, apply adhesive powder, and cure it. You get a ready-to-press transfer that works on cotton, polyester, blends, nylon, leather, and more. No weeding. No pretreatment. Just peel, press at 300-325°F for 10-15 seconds, and you're done. It's the most versatile transfer method on the market.

What's the turnaround time?

Standard orders ship within 1-3 business days from our Virginia production facility. Rush options are available for tight deadlines. We ship nationwide via USPS and UPS, with tracking provided on every order. Need it yesterday? Reach out and we'll see what we can do — we're mountain folk, we hustle.

How do I apply DTF transfers?

It's dead simple. Position the transfer on your garment, press at 300-325°F with medium pressure for 10-15 seconds. Let it cool until warm, then peel. For best results, do a quick 5-second re-press after peeling. Works with a heat press or even a household iron in a pinch (press recommended for consistent results). No pretreatment, no special garments required.

What's a gang sheet?

A gang sheet is a single transfer sheet with multiple designs arranged together to maximize space. You can mix different designs, sizes, and quantities on one sheet. You arrange your designs within the sheet dimensions (46" or 22" wide), and we print the whole thing as one sheet. It's the most cost-effective way to order — fill every inch and pay per sheet, not per design.

Do I need a minimum order?

Absolutely not. Order one sheet or one hundred — same quality, same service, same pricing. We believe in making professional DTF transfers accessible to everyone, from side-hustle crafters pressing in their garage to full production shops running hundreds of shirts a day. One sheet, one thousand sheets — we don't care, we'll print it.

What fabrics work with DTF?

Almost everything. DTF transfers adhere to cotton, polyester, cotton-poly blends, nylon, denim, leather, canvas, and more. Light fabrics, dark fabrics, doesn't matter — the white ink base layer means your colors pop on anything. It's the most fabric-versatile transfer method available, which is exactly why it's taken the custom apparel industry by storm.

How durable are the transfers?

DTF transfers are built to last. With proper application, expect 50+ washes without cracking, peeling, or fading. Wash inside-out in cold water and tumble dry low for maximum longevity. Our transfers use premium inks and adhesive powder that bonds into the fabric fibers, creating a soft, flexible print that moves with the garment. These aren't your grandma's iron-ons.
